## Soft furnishings amplify visual fatigue

Pillows and bedding are seen at close range and for long periods.
Busy designs tend to feel tiring rather than comforting.

Calm symbolic designs work better because they:
- reduce visual noise
- remain readable without demanding attention
- support rest rather than stimulation

## Why structure matters more than detail

On fabric surfaces:
- fine detail blurs
- contrast spreads
- patterns repeat quickly

Symbolic forms with clear structure hold their shape and meaning even when softened by fabric.

## Neutral palettes suit shared spaces

Bedrooms and living spaces are often shared.
Neutral, stone-inspired palettes:
- feel less personal in a good way
- adapt to changing decor
- remain calm across lighting conditions

## Coordinated softness without matching sets

Soft furnishings benefit from coordination, not duplication.
When designs share tone and geometry, pillows and throws feel related without looking staged.

## What usually fails on soft furnishings

Designs struggle when they:
- rely on sharp edges
- use loud contrast
- feel novelty-based

Symbolic restraint avoids these issues.
