## What “Neo‑Deco” means (without the costume)

Classic Art Deco can feel loud if it’s copied literally. The updated direction is different: **clean geometry**, **sculptural structure**, and **selective metallic accents** used with restraint. Think “confident” rather than “ornate.”

This is why Neo‑Deco pairs well with modern symbolic art: both rely on structure as the premium signal.

## The three elements that make it work

### 1) Geometry that breathes
The best modern Deco doesn’t crowd the frame. It uses spacing and symmetry so the design reads instantly—especially important for thumbnails and product previews.

### 2) Selective metallic cues (not glitter)
Gold/brass accents work best when:
- they’re thin, controlled, and used as punctuation
- the surrounding palette is calm (stone tones, rich neutrals)
- contrast is controlled

### 3) Material grounding (stone, linen, wood)
Neo‑Deco looks most premium when paired with material cues that feel real: stone texture, warm neutrals, linen-like softness.

## How to choose Neo‑Deco art for your space

### Choose your lane
- **Warm Neo‑Deco:** camel/taupe + brass cues + travertine tones
- **Cool Neo‑Deco:** charcoal/grey marble + chrome-like highlights
- **Dark Neo‑Deco:** black + controlled gold accents (gift-friendly)

### Match scale to the wall
Neo‑Deco thrives at medium-to-large scale because structure is part of the impact.

## Where Neo‑Deco performs best on products

Because it’s structure-led, Neo‑Deco performs reliably across:
- stickers (centered medallions)
- phone cases (premium-feel surface)
- apparel (clean emblem placement)
- posters/art prints (architectural presence)
