## Texture is a trust signal

Perfectly flat visuals can feel disposable.
Texture feels grounded.

Stone and marble-inspired surfaces read as calmer because they introduce depth without clutter and remain interesting without demanding attention.

## Why natural irregularity ages better

Natural textures carry variation.
That variation prevents a piece from feeling locked into a short-lived trend.

Good textured designs usually:
- avoid harsh digital sharpness
- use controlled contrast
- keep the focal form readable at distance

## Texture versus colour trends

Colour trends move quickly.
Material references move slowly.

Stone, mineral, and marble cues tend to remain relevant through:
- repainting
- furniture changes
- seasonal styling shifts

## When texture becomes noise

Texture should support the symbol—not replace it.

Avoid designs where:
- texture overwhelms structure
- detail collapses at thumbnail size
- contrast becomes harsh or busy

## How texture supports small products

Texture that is restrained holds up across:
- stickers
- phone cases
- apparel placement

Clarity at small sizes matters as much as room presence.
